## Key Topics Covered
The video explores how **Obsidian** (a Markdown-based note-taking app) is used to create an **Agentic Workflow**, leveraging AI (like Claude) for productivity, documentation, and automation. The presenter, **Kyrylo Sulimovskyi**, discusses:

### 1. **Why Markdown Files?**
   - 3 reasons why Markdown is superior to alternatives like Notion or task trackers for AI workflows.
   - Comparison: **HTML vs. Markdown** for AI readability.

### 2. **Obsidian and Its Graph Feature**
   - What is Obsidian and why its **knowledge graph** is critical for AI.
   - How the graph helps AI connect and contextualize information from multiple sources.

### 3. **Plugins for Obsidian**
   - **DataView**: Query and display data dynamically.
   - **Excalidraw**: Create diagrams and visual notes.
   - **Mermaid**: Generate diagrams as code for AI interpretation.

### 4. **Documentation for AI**
   - **TeamHub**: A pet project with AI-friendly documentation.
   - **Architecture Decision Records (ADR)**: Structured records for tracking architectural choices.
   - **Mermaid Diagrams as Code**: How AI reads and processes architectural diagrams.

### 5. **Practical Scenarios (Agentic Workflow Examples)**
   - **Scenario 1**: Task tracker + **Telegram digest** (automated daily summaries via cron, Python, and Redmine).
   - **Scenario 2**: **MacWhisper** for transcribing meetings, classifying them with AI, and auto-creating tasks in Jira.
   - **Scenario 3**: **LLM Wiki** (inspired by Andrej Karpathy’s gist) – turning bookmarks into a searchable knowledge base.
   - **Scenario 4**: **Telegram router `/note`** – a single command to distribute notes to Obsidian, Focus To-Do, or Jira.

### 6. **Advanced Features**
   - **Hybrid Search**: Combining vector, lexical, and ranker-based search locally.
   - **Skill Lint**: Weekly validation of the knowledge base.
   - **History File**: How AI learns from your habits via the last 20 corrections.
   - **Distributed Second Brain**: Syncing Obsidian notes across devices (laptop, VPS, phone) using **Git**.
